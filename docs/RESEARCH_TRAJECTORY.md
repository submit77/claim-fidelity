# Research trajectory

This document distinguishes the checked artifact, the proposed empirical project, and two
conditional extensions. It is a roadmap with stopping conditions, not an expansion of the
repository's current claim surface.

## 1. Present apparatus — Claim Fidelity

The repository supplies:

- formal predicates for scope, standing, consequence licensing, route composition, and
  metadata preservation;
- versioned compiler-record schemas and executable guardrails;
- positive, warning, and refusal fixtures;
- a compact demonstration in which machine validity passes while the attempted consequence
  is refused;
- a reproducible verification gate.

Its claim ceiling remains local: the machinery checks declared structures and invariants. It
does not establish that every relevant feature of an external system has been faithfully
encoded.

## 2. Immediate empirical project — Faithfulness Under Verifiable Reward

The proposed study asks which oversight configuration remains effective as a capable generator
increasingly optimizes against a formally verifiable proxy. Its first wedge is compiling Lean
statement–proof packages paired with natural-language claims.

The core result is not a benchmark score. It is a failure-and-control map: which mismatch
mechanisms appear, where they enter the system, which probes distinguish them, and which
safeguards continue to bind under optimization pressure.

## 3. Conditional extension — controlled rule worlds

Randomized, Lean-checked rule systems would make several rival-capacity dimensions measurable
by construction:

- exposure and rule familiarity;
- retrieval depth;
- search and token budget;
- proof depth and branching factor;
- context availability;
- distribution shift;
- opportunity for template reuse.

**Licensed if:** the core study identifies rival mechanism classes that make distinct,
intervention-sensitive predictions.

**Deprioritized if:** the proposed rivals cannot be operationalized without importing the same
semantic judgments the experiment is intended to test, or if the core study finds no stable
claim–verifier gap under meaningful optimization pressure.

**Potential value:** a controlled testbed for mechanism attribution in formal reasoning, with
capacity tiers declared before evaluation rather than inferred after surprising performance.

## 4. Conditional extension — strategic-behavior audits

The same discipline could be applied to open evaluations of planning, scheming, deception,
situational awareness, or goal-directed behavior. The initial unit would be a methods-focused
reanalysis, not a claim to settle whether models possess any of those properties.

For each consequential attribution, the audit would require:

- the behavioral claim and its operational proxy;
- the strongest capacity-bounded rival explanations;
- predicted dissociations and interventions;
- the system boundary at which the proposed mechanism is located;
- a claim–mechanism–control card identifying candidate safeguards and residual risk.

**Licensed if:** the core method exposes attribution errors that change predicted failure modes
or safeguard selection.

**Deprioritized if:** competing mechanisms make no distinguishable predictions at available
experimental resolution, or the reanalysis cannot be grounded in sufficiently open artifacts.

**Potential value:** better calibrated safety decisions in domains where both over-attribution
and under-attribution are costly.

## Relationship among the names

- **Claim Fidelity** — the present checked apparatus.
- **Compiles ≠ Justifies** — the first experimental wedge.
- **Faithfulness Under Verifiable Reward** — the proposed four-month research program.

This progression preserves provenance without treating the private research program's original
name as the public artifact's identity.
